tk71-00-00-200-801Technical Change: Added procedure for the use of the Commercial Engine Service Memorandum of the On-Wing Engine Maintenance Program.
 TASK 71-00-00-200-801
 1.General.
 A.This section describes the Commercial Engine Service Memorandum (CESM-002) for the powerplant (71-00-00), On-Wing Engine Maintenance Program.
 2.Procedure.
 A.The On-Wing Engine Maintenance Program gives GE Aviation's recommended scheduled inspection and servicing intervals applicable to CF34-10A engines installed on the Bombardier airframes. The On-Wing Engine Maintenance Program consists of the items that follow:
 (1)Repetitive visual and borescope inspections of the external and internal components of the installed engine.
 (2)Specific maintenance checks and tasks.
 (3)Compliance of life limit requirements. Note the items that follow:
 (a)The inspection and maintenance intervals in this section are subject to the life limits or hourly parts limits as described in TASK 05-11-00-200-802 (05-11-00, Airworthiness Limitations - Time Limits 001) and TASK 05-21-00-200-802 (05-21-00, Airworthiness Limitations - Mandatory Inspection 001).
 (b)Replacement intervals for life-limited parts take priority on all on-wing engine maintenance actions.
 (4)Manufacturer's Service Bulletin compliance.
 (5)Performance trend monitoring.
 (6)Exposed hardware inspection requirements.
 B.The maintenance recommendations referenced in this section are in accordance with FAA Advisory Circular 121-22 and ATA Specification MSG-3. Data collection and statistical methods used are based upon the statistical procedures specified in MSG-3 process.
 C.The on-wing maintenance recommendation details are found in the CESM-002. This document, available on the Customer Web Center, is readily accessible to all operators of CF34-10A engines. Questions regarding this CESM or others, please contact CF34 Product Support Engineering, GE Aviation.
