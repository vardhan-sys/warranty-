2.Introduction
The Introduction provides an explanation of the overall purpose and scope of the Illustrated Parts Catalog. Information is provided on the use of the catalog, the standard numbering system, and the GE Aviation (GEA) part numbering system.
3.Numerical Index
A.Contents.
The index is a complete list of all parts included in the detailed parts list. For ease in locating part numbers, the Numerical Index is divided into two lists:
(1)List of alpha part numbers that contains all part numbers that start with a letter (A through Z). This list begins on ALPHA page 1.
(2)List of numeric part numbers that contains all part numbers that start with a number (0 through 9). This list begins on NUMERIC page 1.
(3)Both lists include engine manufacturer part numbers and vendor part numbers, where applicable, for cross-referencing.
B.Arrangement of Columns.
(1)PART NUMBER Column.
(a)Part numbers are listed in alphanumeric order.
(b)A part listed as NO NUMBER in the part number column of the detailed parts list is listed alphabetically in the Numerical Index by the principal noun, or noun phrase, of its nomenclature.
(2)AIRLINE PART NO. Column. This column is reserved for use by the customer.
(3)CH-SECT-UNIT-FIG ITEM Column. This column identifies the location of part numbers in the Detailed Parts List by Chapter-Section-Unit-Figure and Item Number.
(4)TTL REQ Column. This column:
(a)Indicates total quantity of parts for the figure and item number listed.
(b)Identifies disposition of the part by use of abbreviations ALT, AR, and RF. Abbreviations are defined in paragraph 6 of the INTRODUCTION.
C.Use of Symbols and Suffixes.
(1)A dash (-) is used in the CH-SECT-UNIT-FIG ITEM column of the NUMERICAL INDEX to indicate that a part is not illustrated in the figure.
(2)Alpha suffixes are assigned to item numbers consecutively (A through Z) to indicate alternate parts or to indicate parts that supersede or replace former parts.
4.Detailed Parts List.
A.Contents.
(1)Illustrations. The detailed parts list contains illustrations of parts and assemblies.
(2)Parts List. The parts list provides a breakdown of parts required for maintaining the engine.
B.Standard Numbering System.
(1)The numbering system used in the detailed parts list is based on ATA Specification. A standard number is composed of three elements: Chapter, Section, and Unit.
C.Arrangement of columns.
(1)FIG. ITEM Column.
Assemblies or parts not illustrated will be identified by a dash (-) preceding the item number (Example: -210). Letters A through Z, when used as a suffix after the item number, denote an alternate part, or a part that supersedes or replaces former parts. (Example: -210A).
(2)PART NUMBER Column.
Numbers listed in this column are either GEAE, Government Standard, Industry Standard, or Vendor Part Numbers. Some parts may have no part number assigned; these parts, listed as NO NUMBER in the PART NUMBER column, are not procurable. Parts listed as NHA in the PART NUMBER column have a link to the next higher assembly where the next higher assembly part number is identified. The GEAE system of numbering parts reflects certain drawing information; for example:
Figure 1
(a)Generally, a basic part number consists of four digits, a letter, and two more digits. This basic part number identifies the engineering drawing.
(b)The letter G, following the basic part number, stands for GROUP. It means that the part is an assembly. The letter P, following the basic part number, stands for PART and indicates that the part has no further breakdown.
(c)The last two numbers identify one part from other parts that have the same basic drawing number.
NOTE:When ordering a part, use the part number listed in the Part Number column. On the order, give a complete description of the part as listed in the Nomenclature column.
(3)NOMENCLATURE Column:
This column includes appropriate nomenclature and descriptive information necessary to adequately describe the part. Wording is arranged so that the identifying noun, noun phrase, or key word is listed first. Entries are indented to indicate assembly relationship.
Parts that are listed in the Nomenclature column and that require special attention are listed below:
(a)Matched Parts. Two or more parts that would normally be procured as separate items that have been matched to provide a specific functional requirement and for which replacement as a matched set or pair is essential, the term MATCHED PART ORDER NHA is stated in the Nomenclature column. This means that although part numbers and descriptions of the component parts are listed, it will be necessary to replace the next higher assembly.
(b)Spare Part Drawing. Parts or assemblies that differ from production parts and that are used to support engines in the field, are listed as (SPD) in the Nomenclature column.
(c)Non provisioned Parts. Parts that are not provisioned as spare parts are indicated by NP in the Nomenclature column. The parts are listed in the Illustrated Parts Catalog only to clarify assembly relationship.
(d)SIN (Significant Item Number) from the engineering parts list A SIN represents a unique parts location on the engine. SINs are organized by engine module and section. The SIN can be helpful when contacting General Electric for support.

(5)USAGE CODE Column.
(a)The Detailed Parts List contains a column designated USAGE CODE. Alpha codes will be used in the column to identify specific parts which must be used with other specific parts within the same assembly (or installation) and the same engine model. Before applying the "USAGE CODE", ensure the parts are coded for use on the same engine model. See Model Apply Code, this introduction. If the part is not coded for the same engine model, the usage code does not apply.
Within an installation (Figure), a part number identified by a "USAGE CODE" (a single lower case alpha designation) denotes that the affected part can only be used with other parts identified with the same single alpha designation. It also denotes that the affected part can be used with all other non-coded parts (no alpha designation).
When a combination of alphas is used (such as "abd"), the affected parts may be used with all other non-coded parts and all other parts coded "a" or "b" or "d" or any combination thereof (such as "ab" or "ad" or "bd" or "abd"). A part coded "abd" cannot be used with any coded part number which does not contain "a" or "b" or "d" in the alpha combination for the same item.
USAGE CODES are unique to a specific figure. An alpha code may be repeated from figure to figure, but is only to be used for the identification of affected parts within its related figure, (i.e. USAGE CODE "a" for 72-54-00 figure 1 does not relate to USAGE CODE "a" for 72-54-00 figure 5).
If interchangeability of assemblies, sub-assemblies and detail parts is affected between Chapter/Section/Unit/Figure, "USE WITH" indications will be used in the nomenclature column for the affected items.
Fully interchangeable parts do not require USAGE CODE designation, or notes, within the assembly level they support.
(6)UNITS PER ASSY Column.
The number in this column indicates the total quantity of the listed parts required in the immediate assembly. The abbreviations ALT, AR, and RF are used in place of the quantity to further identify the part. Definitions of the abbreviations can be found in the GLOSSARY.
5.How to Use the Catalog.
A.To locate a part when the part number is known, use the GE tech data search utility to search for the part number, or find the part number in the numerical index.
To locate a part when the part number is not known, expand the table of contents and refer to the figure that contains the main group where the part number should be located. Refer to any subgroups as necessary to find the part.
B.IPC part number data is displayed side-by-side (artwork and part information). This is to allow ease of use when locating a given Catalog Sequence Number (CSN).
C.The part number data can be filtered by model, SB Effectivity, and Usage Code. This feature hides non-relevent part numbers based on the filter selection.
6.Standard Parts.
Expendable hardware, having a relatively high usage and application, is normally available from industrial supply outlets. Expendable hardware includes such items as nuts, bolts, screws, fasteners, rivets, seals, etc.
Standard items are identified by the following Part Number types and CAGE codes: ANXX etc., ASXXXXX, ASXXXX-XX etc., MXX etc., MSXX etc., NASXX etc.
