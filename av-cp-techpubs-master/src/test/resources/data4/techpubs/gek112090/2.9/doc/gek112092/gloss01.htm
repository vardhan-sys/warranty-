EIPC DEFINITIONS OF ABBREVIATIONS, TERMS, AND SYMBOLS.
TERMDEFINITION
ALTAlternate:Parts may be used interchangeably.
ARAs Required: Use this item in the amount necessary to complete a particular job.
ASSYAssembly
CMMComponent Maintenance Manual
DEL BY SBDeleted by Service Bulletin: Used for all parts removed by service bulletin. Refer to service bulletin for disposition of affected parts.
DELDeleted: Parts or assembly no longer required for use but not necessarily superseded or replaced by another part or assembly.
DETDetails
FIGFigure
FWDForward
GEAEGE Aircraft Engines
LHLeft-Hand
MATCHED ASSYThis identifes a part which is a matched assembly. None of the components are interchangeable with components of other parts having the same or different part numbers.
MATCHED PARTThis identifies parts that are provisioned separately, but which require matching to a mating part.
MATCHED PART ORDER NHAThis indicates a part that is not provisioned separately; the next higher assembly must be ordered.
NHANext Higher Assembly
NOTEThis is used when additional parts information is necessary to further explain the usage of parts.
NPNot provisioned
RThe letter "R" in the margin of the detailed parts list is a symbol for line changes or additions.
REPL BYReplaced By: This indicates parts that are fully interchangeable. Old parts are used until supply is exhausted.
RFThe letters RF in the Units Per Assembly column indicate that the listing of the part or assembly is repeated, and that reference should be made to its appropriate listing in another figure.
RHRight-Hand
RWKRework
SBService Bulletin: The only official document used to notify customers of one-time modifications to delivered engines or engine parts.
SEERefer to figure indicated. Part is required in figure to show relationship with other items.
SEE CMMThis term directs operator to the component maintenance or accessory manual that provides instructions for that component or accessory.
SEE FOR DETThis references the figure in which the assembly is divided into its component parts.
SEE FOR NHAThis references the figure in which the relationship of the part to its next higher assembly can be found.
SINSIN (Significant Item Number) number from the engineering parts list. A SIN represents a unique location on the engine.
SPDSpare Parts Application Drawing. This term identifies a part (or parts) that differs from the production part (or parts) and is only used in repairs.
SUPSD BYSuperseded By: This indicates non interchangeability of parts.
USE WITHThis indicates that the part must be used with a part located in another figure.
-Item is not illustrated.
