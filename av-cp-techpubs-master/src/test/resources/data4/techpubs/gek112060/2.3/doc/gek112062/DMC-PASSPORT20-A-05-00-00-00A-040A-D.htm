  • The ALS data in section 05-11 has been substantiated to the Certificating Authority based on engineering analysis that assumes this product will be operated and maintained using the configurations, procedures, and inspections in the Instructions for Continued Airworthiness (ICAs) supplied with this product by the Type Certificate holder. For LLPs, and parts that influence LLPs, any repair, alteration, or other maintenance not technically substantiated by the Type Certificate holder (or its licensees) or any replacement of such parts not supplied by the Type Certificate holder (or its licensees) can introduce differences that may materially affect these limits and warrant additional analysis and approval of the Certificating Authority.

  • For the purpose of this manual, influencing parts that affect LLPs are defined as LLP-influencing parts. A list of LLP-influencing parts is identified in paragraph 5. The list is consistent with the part categorization process described in FAA Advisory Circulars 33-8 (Paragraph 5 and Appendix 1) and 33-9 (Paragraph 5 and Appendix 1). LLP-influencing parts are either Category 1 or Category 2 parts when using this process.

    Note:  The list of LLP-influencing parts identified in paragraph 5. is not an all-inclusive list of influencing parts that can adversely impact LLP integrity. Parts on the list were selected using an agency-accepted identification process and are parts that experience has shown may directly or indirectly influence the boundary conditions of the lifting system used to determine the LLP Airworthiness Limitations. The absence of parts from this list does not necessarily mean that repair, alteration, or replacement of such parts cannot produce systems effects or adversely affect LLP integrity.

  • In addition to the technical and regulatory requirement guidance provided by FAA Advisory Circulars 33-8 and 33-9, any repair, alteration, or other maintenance to LLP-influencing parts also requires appropriate assessments of such repair, alteration, or other maintenance being conducted on the engine system as a whole in order to establish the effect on LLPs with regard to compliance with agency prescribed integrity requirements.

6. LLP-Influencing Parts.

  • Table 2 identifies the LLP-influencing parts which are those parts associated with each LLP in Table 1 that can directly or indirectly influence LLP integrity. Direct influences are considered to be form or fit, that is, they are based on physical contact or interface clearances between adjacent parts. Indirect influences are functional in nature, these influences are not based on physical contact, but may include aerodynamics, thermal, and vibratory effects, etc. Repair, alteration, replacement, or other maintenance applied to LLP-influencing parts may affect engine performance, operability, thermal profiles, primary and secondary flow behavior, stress distribution, vibration modes (including aeroelastic considerations), and material characteristics, including damage tolerance considerations.

  • To be sure that the ALS data in Section 05-11 remains valid, LLP-influencing parts must be maintained in a manner consistent with the inspection and repair procedures in Chapter 72 of the engine manufacturer’s ICAs.

    Note:  Only the primary Catalog Sequence Number (CSN) for each LLP-influencing part is given. Alternative parts (identified with a suffix) that follow the primary CSN in the Illustrated Parts Catalog (IPC) are also LLP-influencing parts.
