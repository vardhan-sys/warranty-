3. Summary

A.  Summary
This service bulletin introduces a lacing procedure for the W8, W100, W200, W300 and W400 electrical harnesses to decrease unwanted movement and to prevent any contact.
4. Planning Information

A.  Applicability
This service bulletin is applicable to these engines: 904124 thru 904140.
This service bulletin has been introduced in production to these engines: 904141 and up.
These Serial Numbers are the best available data.

B.  Concurrent requirements
Not applicable.

C.  Reason

  1. Objective:

    To make maintainability better and decrease harness movement.

  2. Condition:

    Fan compartment harnesses move and catch at selected locations, which leads to possible contact or clash with adjacent hardware.

  3. Cause:

    Harnesses are loose and do not have enough clearance at certain locations.

  4. Improvement:

    Harnesses are bundled with lacing tape to prevent relative motion and increase clearances.

  5. Substantiation:

    Comparative analysis.

D.  Description
This service bulletin introduces a lacing procedure to bundle together the W8, W100, W200, W300 and W400 right and left-hand electrical harnesses to decrease unwanted movement and to prevent any contact.

E.  Compliance
Category 7

6.   Accomplishment Instructions

A. General.
Note:  Lacing and bundling applies to the W200, W300, and W8 harnesses on right and left-hand engines, and the W100 and W400 harnesses for right-hand engines only.
(1)

This procedure gives instructions to apply lacing tape to these electrical harnesses. Refer to Figure 1.

  • W8 fan harness (black/gray/red): left-hand (10-011, 73-21-51) and right-hand (15-011, 73-21-51)

  • W100 harness (black/gray/violet): right-hand only (25-031, 73-21-51)

  • W200 harness (black/gray/orange): left-hand (30-191, 73-21-51) and right-hand (35-191, 73-21-51)

  • W300 harness (black/gray/green): left-hand (40-211, 73-21-51) and right-hand (45-221, 73-21-51)

  • W400 harness (black/gray/blue): right-hand only (55-031, 73-21-51)
(2)

Do not do this procedure unless you are a approved technician.

(3)

Obey all of the applicable safety and environmental controls or injury to personnel can occur.

(4)

Do an inspection of all the engine parts that you can see when you do maintenance. Examine all the removed parts that you will install again. Examine all the parts that are not removed, but that you can see during maintenance.

(5)

Get access to the upper fan cowl area.
(6)

Open only those breakers that are affected.
B.

Installation of lacing tape and bundle procedure.

(1)

Apply lacing tape to the W200 and W300 harnesses on right-hand and left-hand engines near the accessory gearbox fire detector as follows. Refer to Figure 2.

Note:  The surface of the terminal overbraid must be free of foreign matter that would prevent tape bond.
(a)

Wind the terminal pairs with PTFE-coated aramid lacing (PN A22C7A1).
(b)

Use a single continuous piece of tape. Wind it 2 to 3 times centered at the installation reference mark.

Note:  Do not spiral wrap.
(c)

Push tape firmly to get complete contact around the bundle, as there must be no visual indication of areas not bonded.

Note:  Tears are not permitted.
(d)

Lace the terminal pairs that you wind with two laces of tape, lacing and tying, aramid (PN A-A-52084-C-3).
(e)

Wind each lace 4 to 5 times at the installation reference mark and tie with a knot for tape retention. Do not tighten too much.
(f)

Cut the excess length 0.25”- 0.50” from the knots of each lace.
(g)

Lace terminal pairs of the same harness together, approximately midspan of the shortest terminal.
(h)

When you bundle, do not let the terminal lugs be pulled. Do not bundle terminals of the adjacent harness.
(i)

After lacing, bundle must maintain a minimum clearance of 0.125” inch to any adjacent hardware.
(j)

Do steps 6.B.(1)(a) thru 6.B.(1)(j) again to bundle the W200 and W300 harnesses together.
(2)

Apply lacing tape to the two W8 fan harnesses on right-hand and left-hand engines near the air turbine starter as follows. Refer to Figure 3.

(a)

Do steps 6.B.(1)(a) thru 6.B.(1)(j) to apply lacing tape to the two W8 fan harnesses.
(b)

Do steps 6.B.(1)(a) thru 6.B.(1)(j) again to bundle the two W8 fan harnesses together.
(3)

Apply lacing tape to the W100 and W400 harnesses on right-hand engines near the permanent magnet generator (PMG) and variable frequency generator (VFG) as follows. Refer to Figure 3.

(a)

Do steps 6.B.(1)(a) thru 6.B.(1)(j) to apply lacing tape to the W100 and W400 harnesses.
(b)

Do steps 6.B.(1)(a) thru 6.B.(1)(j) again to bundle the W100 and W400 harnesses together.
(4)

Make sure that all breakers that were opened are closed.
