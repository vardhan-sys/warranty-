C.  Reason

  1. Objective:

    To better reliability, decrease significant events, and to decrease the cost of ownership.

  2. Condition:

    This service bulletin gives a consolidated list by GE Aviation of recommended inspections and servicing tasks applicable to installed Passport 20 engines. The purpose is for maintenance planning only.

  3. Cause:

    This service bulletin gives a consolidated list of recommended inspection and servicing tasks.

  4. Inspection:

    The adoption of inspection recommendations and servicing task makes the product reliability better and decreases significant events.

  5. Substantiation:

    Comparative analysis.

    Substantiation is completed based on the initial maintenance inspection requirements, and by Transport Canada approved Bombardier Global 7500 Maintenance Review Board Report (BD700-3AB48-11400-02) which used the Maintenance Steering Group (MSG-3) analysis process.

6.   Accomplishment Instructions

A. General.
(1)

These on-wing recommendations are based on operation in typical revenue service. If the engines are operated in unusual service or in dangerous environmental conditions, the operator must start a customized inspection and servicing plan. Pilot training and high cycle-to-hour service are examples of conditions that could be considered as unusual service. Operation during sandstorms, and on underdeveloped airstrips, etc., are examples of detrimental environmental conditions. These inspection intervals are GE recommendations. The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA), Transport Canada (TC), European Aviation Safety Agency (EASA), Maintenance Review Board (MRB), Airframer, and all other regulatory authorities have precedence over these recommendations. Operators should use primary publications which include, but are not limited to the MPD, Instructions for Continued Airworthiness (ICA) documentation, Service Bulletins, etc., to get the most current information. When more than one scheduling criteria is listed, do the action that occurs first.
